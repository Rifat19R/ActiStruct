# ActiStruct v0.5.1 Release Note

## Version / Milestone

v0.5.1 — repeated-trial offline stress benchmark for failure-aware GP/LCB
acquisition.

## Date

2026-06-27

## Summary

v0.5.0 ran a single offline trial on the full natural candidate pool and
found that `lcb_only` already selected zero known failures at top-10, which
left no room to show whether failure-aware re-ranking actually reduces
failed selections relative to LCB-only. v0.5.1 repeats the comparison across
50 trials and four harder candidate-pool conditions (`normal_pool`,
`failure_enriched_pool`, `heldout_material_pool`, `high_uncertainty_pool`),
so genuine LCB-only failures can occur and be compared against. This is an
offline simulation using completed records; no QE/DFT jobs were run.

## Main Result

At top_k=10, mean ± std over 50 trials, `failure_aware_aggressive` vs
`lcb_only`:

| Pool mode | Failures (LCB-only → aggressive) | Risk (LCB-only → aggressive) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `normal_pool` | 1.48±1.33 → 0.30±0.54 | 0.163±0.038 → 0.096±0.027 |
| `failure_enriched_pool` | 3.86±1.74 → 1.80±1.67 | 0.193±0.036 → 0.115±0.036 |
| `heldout_material_pool` | 0.74±1.98 → 0.62±1.51 | 0.129±0.078 → 0.103±0.070 |
| `high_uncertainty_pool` | 0.00±0.00 → 0.06±0.24 | 0.169±0.043 → 0.120±0.026 |

Mean predicted failure risk was reduced in all four pool modes. Known
failed-selection counts were reduced clearly in `normal_pool` and
`failure_enriched_pool`; the `heldout_material_pool` reduction was smaller
than its own trial-to-trial noise, and `high_uncertainty_pool` showed no
failure-count improvement.

## Conservative Safe Claim

> ActiStruct v0.5.1 extends the v0.5.0 offline benchmark into repeated
> stress tests. Across 50 trials, failure-aware LCB reduced the mean
> predicted failure risk in all tested pool modes. It also reduced known
> failed selections relative to LCB-only most clearly in normal and
> failure-enriched pools, while behavior was weaker in heldout-material
> pools and not universally better in high-uncertainty pools. These results
> support failure risk as a soft DFT triage signal, not a guarantee of live
> DFT savings.

## Limitations

- Offline simulation on completed records only; no new QE/PBE validation.
- `predicted_value` remains a constant 0.0 placeholder, so ranking
  differences come from the uncertainty proxy and failure-risk penalty, not
  a live predicted-energy signal.
- Failure-risk estimates are inherited from v0.3.2 and carry large
  split-to-split variance on held-out materials.
- `heldout_material_pool` and `high_uncertainty_pool` did not show a clear
  failure-count improvement.
- No live DFT savings have been demonstrated; this remains an offline
  triage-signal result.

## Tests

## QE/DFT

No QE/DFT jobs were performed for this release. All v0.5.1 results are
offline simulations over previously completed QE reliability records and
v0.3.2 failure-risk predictions.

## Next Recommended Step

Design and run a small live GP/QE active-learning trial with failure-aware
acquisition enabled, on a system with known, reachable failure modes, to
test whether the offline risk-reduction signal observed here holds up under
live DFT execution. No v0.6/GNN feature work until that live validation step
is complete.
